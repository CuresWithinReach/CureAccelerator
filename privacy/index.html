Privacy Policy

Effective January 23, 2015

CureAccelerator (www.CureAccelerator.org) (“Site”) is a community web site. Our goal is to provide a platform for stakeholders who want to share their knowledge and resources to create repurposed treatments for unsolved diseases. We know our success in achieving this goal depends on a shared belief in community, transparency, philanthropy, science, clinical care and empathy. Being transparent about medical research science and finance is not for everyone, and we strive, with full transparency, to outline the benefits and risks of being part of this site, including those related to privacy.

This Privacy Policy outlines the type of information CureAccelerator collects from individuals who have registered to join CureAccelerator (“Users”) and how it is shared and is not shared with other Users and with third parties.

We reserve the right to modify this policy at any time, and without prior notice, by posting an amended Privacy Policy and terms on this website. We encourage Users to review this policy periodically for any updates.

What kind of information we collect:

Shared Data

Some of the information that Users provide about themselves may be shared with the CureAccelerator community and others (“Shared Data”). Examples of Shared Data that Users may submit at the Site, including through their profile (“User Profile”), may include:

  • Biographical information, e.g. photograph, biography, gender, age, location (city, state and country), general notes;
  • Health information;
  • Treatment information, e.g. treatment start dates, stop dates, dosages, side effects, treatment evaluations;
  • Symptom information, e.g. severity, duration;
  • Science information, e.g. research data and insights, research project ideas, scientific reviews of research;
  • Clinical information, e.g. non-confidential clinical protocols, on-label and off-label treatment recommendations, research project ideas, clinical reviews of research;
  • Financial information, e.g. funding pledges and commitments, historical personal or organizational financial information;
  • Information shared via free text fields, e.g. discussion forums, Requests for Proposals, Letters of Intent, Full Grants, Reviews, surveys, online postings, annotations, journals, feeds and other text fields and documents; and
  • Connections to other people on the Site, e.g. invitations to join, to review, to communicate, including emails, text messages and other forms of online communication.

In the course of using the Site, Users may share information that could be used to reasonably identify them (“Personal Information”), including name, photograph, location and email address. When a User chooses to share Personal Information via a free text field and photos or images, the information shall be treated as Shared Data.

Restricted Data

Some of the information that Users enter into certain fields when registering to use or using the Site will not be shared (“Restricted Data”). A User may opt to share is or her Restricted Data with other Users. The type of Restricted Data that Users may submit at the Site may include:

  • Name, as collected as part of registration or in a User’s Account Information;
  • Email address, as collected and verified as part of registration or in a User’s Account Information;
  • Password, as collected as part of registration or in a User’s Account Information;
  • Mailing address; and
  • Private messages.

When a User enters Personal Information, including name and email address, as part of registering to use CureAccelerator, that Personal Information is treated as Restricted Data.

Cookies

A cookie is a small data file that often includes an anonymous, unique token, which is sent to your browser from a website’s computers and may be stored on your computer’s hard drive. Two types of cookies are required to use the Site:

  • Session Cookies are temporary cookies that remain only until you log out of the website or exit your web browser. Session Cookies do not store any Personal Information, only a unique visitor ID number that may be used to ensure Users are properly authenticated and can view site information.
  • Persistent Cookies remain in the cookie file of your browser for much longer, even after you leave the Site. Persistent Cookies also do not store any Personal Information. Persistent Cookies store preferences that should persist from visit to visit, like “Remember Me,” and a unique token to enable us to understand how Users use the Site (“Site Usage”).

Transparency Decree

Effective January 23, 2015

Most User websites have a Privacy Policy. Ours is listed above and we encourage you to read it carefully-it provides a lot of important information.

We also have a Transparency Decree. That may sound like a paradox (not to be confused with a “pair of docs”), but we believe that Transparency is necessary to maximize the ability of Repurposing Research to drive more treatments to more patients more quickly.

There are thousands of generic drugs, devices and nutriceuticals that can be repurposed, in their current dosage and delivery form, to help improve the quality and length of patients’ lives, and to reduce healthcare costs. Unfortunately, there is often no economic incentive for any company or individual to finance the research that would prove these repurposed uses. Proving a generic drug that is inexpensive and widely available could help a rare disease is a noble endeavor, but unlikely to create a financial return on investment, even if that clinical trial proof led only to off-label use, without the additional time and costs involved in securing regulatory marketing approval.

In addition, it is often challenging for researchers and clinicians to disclose their Repurposing Research ideas in public, because they might lose the opportunity to protect intellectual property, secure a grant, publish a paper, increase their practice or move up in the academic ranks. These challenges can often delay or discourage proving these ideas in a way that they can move forward to patients.

CureAccelerator and our supporters believe that the more transparent and altruistic we are, the greater the impact on patients. So, we encourage our Users to be as open, transparent, altruistic, giving, participatory, positive, forthcoming, supportive and connected as possible. Our guiding vision is a medical research and treatment development system through which every possible existing compound, device and treatment resource is used to make patients healthier, in which diverse stakeholders join together to create uncommon results.

Your participation on CureAccelerator can be the catalyst to correct a rampant global healthcare problem-the lack of treatments for unsolved diseases. We believe that CureAccelerator can help drive Repurposing Research in a transformative, rapid, affordable and effective way. It only works if we all participate in this together, as transparently as possible.
